Chapter 3 Solid Textures A texture in graphics usually means a function that makes the colors on a surface procedural. This procedure can be synthesis code, or it could be an image lookup, or a combination of both. We will first make all colors a texture. Most programs keep constant rgb colors and textures different classes so feel free to do something different, but I am a big believer in this architecture because being able to make any color a texture is great. Those checker odd/even pointers can be to a constant texture or to some other procedural texture. This is in the spirit of shader networks introduced by Pat Hanrahan back in the 1980s.
